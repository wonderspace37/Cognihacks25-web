Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about participating in CogniHacks 2025. Can't find what you're looking for? Reach out to us!

Eligibility & Registration

Who can participate in CogniHacks 2025?

CogniHacks 2025 is open to anyone 18 years or older with an interest in neurotechnology and AI. You don't need prior experience with BCI hardware – we welcome beginners! Students, professionals, researchers, and hobbyists are all encouraged to participate.

How do I register for the event?

Registration opens on August 1, 2025. Visit our registration portal and fill out the application form. Registration is free, but space is limited to 200 participants. We recommend registering early as spots fill up quickly!

You'll need to provide: basic contact information, experience level, dietary restrictions, and emergency contact details.

Is there a registration fee?

No! CogniHacks 2025 is completely free to attend. This includes access to all BCI hardware, meals throughout the day, mentorship, and prizes. Our sponsors make this possible.

Can I participate remotely?

CogniHacks 2025 is an in-person only event. Access to specialized BCI hardware and hands-on mentorship requires physical presence. However, we're planning virtual workshops leading up to the event for remote participants to learn about neurotech.

Team Formation

Do I need to form a team before the event?

No! While you can come with a pre-formed team (max 4 people), we'll have a team formation session at 8:30 AM on event day. Many participants find great teammates during this session. You can also work solo if you prefer.

What's the maximum team size?

Teams can have up to 4 members. We find this size works best for hackathon collaboration while ensuring everyone can contribute meaningfully to the project.

How are teams matched during team formation?

We'll have a structured team formation process where participants can pitch ideas, share their skills, and find complementary teammates. We'll also have facilitators to help match people based on interests, experience levels, and chosen tracks.

Schedule & Event Details

What does the full schedule look like?

7:30 AM - Registration & Breakfast
8:30 AM - Opening Ceremony & Team Formation
9:00 AM - Hacking Begins
12:00 PM - Lunch & BCI Hardware Demos
1:00 PM - Continued Hacking
5:00 PM - Project Submissions Due
5:30 PM - Project Presentations
7:30 PM - Awards Ceremony & Closing

Mentors will be available throughout the day, and there will be mini-workshops on BCI programming at 10 AM and 2 PM.

How long do teams have to present their projects?

Each team will have 3 minutes to present followed by 2 minutes for questions from judges. We recommend focusing on the problem you solved, your technical approach, and a live demo if possible.

What happens if I arrive late?

Late arrivals are accommodated until 10 AM, but you'll miss breakfast and team formation. We recommend arriving by 8 AM to get the full experience. Contact us if you expect to be significantly delayed.

What to Bring

What should I bring to the hackathon?

Essential: Laptop, chargers, government-issued ID

Recommended: External monitor, mouse, keyboard, notebooks, pens, phone charger

Optional: Extension cords, pillows/blankets (rest area available), personal snacks

Provided: All BCI hardware, development boards, basic electronics components, WiFi, power outlets

Do I need to install any software beforehand?

We recommend installing basic development tools for your preferred programming language. For BCI development, we suggest:

  • Python 3.8+ with pip
  • Git for version control
  • Code editor (VS Code, PyCharm, etc.)
  • Emotiv SDK (we'll provide installation guides)

Don't worry if you miss something – we'll have setup help available on event day.

BCI Hardware & Technology

What BCI hardware will be available?

We'll have a variety of brain-computer interface devices available:

  • Emotiv EPOC X - 14-channel EEG headset
  • Emotiv Insight - 5-channel consumer EEG
  • NeuroSky MindWave - Single-channel affordable option
  • OpenBCI boards - Open-source EEG platforms
  • Eye-tracking devices - For gaze-based interfaces

Hardware will be shared among teams, with priority given to teams actively using the devices.

Can I use my own BCI hardware?

Absolutely! If you have your own BCI hardware or development boards, you're welcome to bring and use them. Just let us know during registration so we can ensure our mentors are familiar with your setup.

Do I need prior experience with BCI hardware?

Not at all! We'll have beginner-friendly tracks and mentors specifically assigned to help newcomers. We'll provide quick-start guides and have hands-on demos at lunch to get everyone up to speed.

Food & Accommodations

What meals are provided?

All meals and snacks are included:

  • Breakfast: Continental breakfast with coffee, pastries, fruit
  • Lunch: Catered lunch with multiple options
  • Dinner: Pizza and salads
  • Snacks: Available throughout the day (energy bars, coffee, tea, fruit)
Can you accommodate dietary restrictions?

Yes! Please specify any dietary restrictions, allergies, or preferences during registration. We can accommodate vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free, halal, kosher, and other common dietary needs.

Are there sleeping accommodations?

CogniHacks is a single-day event (12.5 hours), so overnight accommodations aren't provided. However, we do have a quiet rest area with couches if you need a quick power nap during the day.

Prizes & Judging

What prizes are available?

Overall Winners:

  • 1st Place: $3,000 + Emotiv EPOC X headsets for team
  • 2nd Place: $2,000 + tech package
  • 3rd Place: $1,000 + tech package

Track-Specific Prizes: Best project in each track wins $500 and specialized hardware

Special Awards: Most Creative, Best Beginner Team, People's Choice

How are projects judged?

Projects are evaluated on:

  • Innovation (30%): Creativity and novelty of the approach
  • Technical Implementation (25%): Quality of code and use of BCI technology
  • Impact Potential (25%): Real-world applicability and user benefit
  • Presentation (20%): Clarity of explanation and demo quality

Our judging panel includes industry professionals, researchers, and previous hackathon winners.
